Mongrelfolk

   The inevitable result of centuries of intermingling between Lycanthropes of every kind, Mongrelfolk are less a distinct species as they are... well, deserving of their name. A Mongrelfolk occurs after generations of different Lycanthropic bloodlines mix at random. The individual bloodlines dilute and mix, frequently in unpredictable ways.
    Mongrelfolk vary widely in appearance. They are always humanoid, however, and usually are not shorter than five feet tall, or taller than seven. All Mongrelfolk have a mix of humanlike and beastial features, usually humanlike faces, oddly colored eyes, and patches of fur, scales, or feathers. Some Mongrelfolk have additional animalistic features, frequently from a single bloodline, but sometimes multiple bloodlines can be apparent.

    Until the formation of the ALT, Mongrelfolk lived off the land in primarily tribal communities. Since the formation of the ALT, hundreds of thousands of them have flocked to that nation to build a home for themselves. They now form the majority of the population in the ALT.
    Mongrelfolk still revere nature, and Druids are the leaders of the community. They tend towards suspicion of those that persecuted them for so long, and tend to enjoy spending time in nature. These days, most Mongrelfolk are as urban and educated as anyone else. Old habits simply die hard.
    Some Mongrelfolk still live in tribal communities, and some have lived in both cities and tribes due to service in the ALT’s unique military.

    Note that not all crossbreeds result in Mongrelfolk, not even breeding between Mongrelfolk and a full blooded Lycanthrope. Cross-species pairings, and mongrel-full pairings usually take the form of the mother. It takes generations of random interbreeding to create mongrelfolk, and as such there is no taboo on intermingling and full blooded natural Lycanthropes are not in danger of being “Bred out.”




Stats

Size: Medium
Type: Humanoid
Speed: 30 (May change- See Aspects)

Abilities: Mongrelfolk are nothing if not adaptable. Their Lycan blood expresses itself in unique ways for practically every individual. They get a +2 bonus to one ability score of their choice. This choice may be limited by their Aspect, see below.

Languages: Mongrelfolk can take any language as their bonus language.

Night Sight: All Mongrelfolk possess low light vision.

Animal Affinity: Though they cannot communicate with animals like their full-blooded peers, they maintain a connection to all animals. Mongrelfolk gain a +2 on Ride and Handle Animal checks, and these skills are always class for them.

Enduring: Mongrelfolk are hardy and resilient, born of generations of hardship. They receive a +4 racial bonus on fortitude and constitution saves versus fatigue and exhaustion, as well as any other negative effects from running, forced marches, starvation, thirst, or hot or cold environments. They may sleep in medium or heavy armor.

Bestial: Though they are humanoids, Mongrelfolk can take any Monstrous feats that they otherwise meet the prerequisites for.


Luna's Blood: Due to their heritage, Mongrelfolk cannot become Afflicted Lycanthropes. They are immune to the Curse of Lycanthropy. 

Aspects: Each Mongrelfolk derives unique physical traits from their bloodlines. They call these Aspects, and each one is different, though they generally fall into the categories below. Each Mongrelfolk gains only one Aspect.

  • Hunt Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains the Scent special ability. These Mongrelfolk are generally humanoid in appearance, though they may possess more animalistic noses, or tongues as the case may be.
  • Swift Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains +10 Speed, +2 Initiative, and gains the benefit of the Run feat. These mongrelfolk have long, often digitigrade legs. This Mongrelfolk must select Dexterity as their ability bonus.
  • Sky Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains a Fly Speed of 30 Feet (Clumsy.) They must take a full run action to take off. They possess large feathery or batlike wings, with around a 15 foot wingspan. This can occasionally cause problems in tight spaces. They must select Dexterity as their ability.
  • Water Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains the amphibious special quality and the aquatic subtype. They gain a Swim Speed of 30 Feet and can always count Swim as a class skill. They gain a +8 bonus on Swim checks, and typically have webbed hands and feet.
  • Fur Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains +3 Natural Armor, and +2 on saves vs. cold (Or heat, if scales.) This Aspect generally expresses as thick fur and a more animalistic appearance, though it can also be thick hide or scales.
  • Ripper aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains two 1d4 Claw attacks and a 1d6 Bite attack. These Mongrelfolk tend to be some of the most bestial in appearance.
  • Blood Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains Dr 5/Silver. This is the second rarest Mongrelfolk trait, and these mongrelfolk tend to be the most bestial in appearance. They are the most connected to their Lycan heritage, and it can sometimes be hard to distinguish between them and a full-blooded lycanthrope.
  • Moon Aspect: This Mongrelfolk gains the ability to see normally in any darkness at any range, including the ability to see through magical darkness. They describe seeing the world as if bathed in moonlight. This is the rarest expression of Mongrelfolk, and they are seen as being blessed by Luna as it is the only ability that not even their full-blooded peers can possess. They are generally the least animalistic in appearance.
  • Runt: Not everyone gets great power from their lineage. Some mongrelfolk are born smaller than their siblings, some barely breaking four feet tall. They face a tough life and are frequently the targets of bullying and hardship, but the school of hard knocks has its own benefits. Size: Small. Base Speed: 30. Runts gain the following abilities: Runts get a +2 Dodge bonus to AC, and a +2 on saving throws vs Fear, and to the DC to intimidate them. They do not suffer a penalty to intimidating creatures up to 2 size categories larger than them.
