Lycans

   Though they are gaining a modicum of acceptance, true Lycans are a rare sight outside of Nova island. However, many Lycans are restless in a stable, modern society. Given their natural skills, Lycans of varying types tend to make excellent specialist soldiers, mercenaries, and adventurers. In particular, they are gaining a reputation as the best trackers in the world. Blackfeather has a surprisingly high number of Lycans of varying types in its ranks. Still, Lycans can still expect to be viewed with suspicion, or sometimes outright fear and hatred depending on where in the world they go.

    Lycanthropy is treated as a fundamental aspect of the creature, for a natural Lycanthrope. Spells that remove afflictions or curses do not affect natural Lycanthropes. Only spells that can actually change the type of the creature can change a natural lycanthrope. Afflicted Lycanthropes are treated as just that- afflicted. If an afflicted lycanthrope is killed, the curse is removed and they revert to their natural form. If a natural lycanthrope is killed, their body stays in the form they were in when they died. If a Lycanthrope corpse is raised as an undead- such as a necropolitan- they are created in the form they were in when the ritual started. In this case, they are permanently attached to this form- they lose the Shapechanger type and cannot change shape.  

Lycanthropes in the rules

    A Lycanthrope's level adjustment is decided like this: CR of the base creature (rounded down) + Base CR of the base animal (rounder down), plus 1.
    So, a human weredeer is calculated like this: Deer (CR 1/2) Rounds down to 0, plus Human (CR 1/2) Rounds down to zero, +1 for Lycanthrope abilities equals total 1. So, level adjustment 1.

    A halfling werewolf would be like this- Wolf (Cr 1) plus Halfling (CR 1/2) = 1, Plus 1 = 2. So, level adjustment 2.

NOTE: This is not always balanced. For the time being please run any Lycan characters past a DM to make sure their level adjustment is calculated correctly.


    A few other notes on this: Given the history of the Lycan nations, the vast majority of Lycans use Human, Elf, Half-elf, or halfling as their base creature. A smattering of Drow and perhaps a few other races exist. Given this, there aren't really any Lycanthropes with exotic base races floating around.

    Important note: If your Lycanthrope's level adjustment comes out to +1, adjust your DR to DR 5/Silver instead of 10/Silver. This can be brought back up with a feat, see Errata.

A Note on Bjargknottr
    Since the founding of the Allied Lycanthropic Tribes, the sport of Bjargknottr has snowballed to surprising fame throughout the world. While it isn’t the largest sport in the world, it is the fastest growing. The brutal, simple, and exciting pace of Bjargknottr has made it a crowd favorite, if an insurance nightmare.
    The basis of Bjargknottr is simple: Get the ball (The Bjargknottr) into the other team’s goal by any means necessary. The game is very light on other rules, mostly no fatal clawing or biting, and you can totally use the ball as a weapon if you want.
    Since not everyone has the durability of a lycanthrope, there are now two leagues of Bjargknottr- Traditional and Regulation. Traditional is still the same game it always was- Light on rules, heavy on rocks. It is still primarily played by Lycanthropes, though other tough magical creatures and dwarves have teams.
    The Regulation league is open to any race. It has a few more rules on safety and fair play, most notable the ball (While still a rock) is heavily padded. It’s still a rock, though, and the game is still relentlessly brutal enough that only the toughest examples of each race need apply.
    In addition, many Adventurers (particularly Lycanthropes) have discovered that a Bjargknotter makes a brutally effective, if awkward, weapon. The Bjargknottr itself is a carefully polished and balanced granite ball, a bit over a foot in diameter, with carved or padded straps for carrying and throwing. Regulation balls are the same size and weight, but covered in leather padding.
