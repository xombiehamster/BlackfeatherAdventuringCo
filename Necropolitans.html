Necropolitans

   For much of history, becoming an Undead was typically viewed with negative connotations. Labels like “monster” and “scourge” and “damned soul” and “unholy abomination of life” were
 commonplace for Undead of an earlier age: it’s little wonder that they more often than not turned out a little bit vengeful. In more enlightened times, however, Undead are beginning to be viewed in a new light: the success of an independent nation of the “post living” has shown that you don’t actually have to be evil to be undead. It is becoming ever more evident that, just because someone runs on Negative energy, it no more guarantees that they are evil than living with positive energy means you are Good. People are people, no matter which way their biological processes do or don’t work. That said, it must be admitted that the transformation to some kinds of undead does have an effect on people... The loss of the organic origin of many moods, desires, and instincts can have a discomforting effect on a psyche. That is why years of research have culminated in the development of a new variety of Undead, one which seeks to provide the basic benefits of Undeath (immortality, no biological functions, etc) without many of the drawbacks(craving blood or other living tissue, being incorporeal, loss of sensation in your everything, etc). The modern Necropolitan, as these undead are called, merely perform a simple ritual which stops their conventional biological processes over the course of a few days, and then restarts them in the way that most corporeal undead enjoy. Although the Necropolitan is well and truly dead, their body continues some basic biological functions which help to avoid some of the loss of ‘mortality’ often experienced by other forms of undead.

    Necropolitans can join the ranks of the undead through means other than deliberate choice. They are sometimes the result of accident or malicious magical intent. Becoming a necropolitan by accident or ill deeds is less common than becoming one by choice, but it does happen. Necropolitan characters can be any alignment, but will always count as Evil for the purposes of spells and special abilities - such as smite evil.

Special: Use Craft(Taxidermy) instead of heal checks for first aid, treating deadly wounds, and treating wounds caused by caltrops, spike growth, or spike stones. Can not be used in place of a heal check for long term care, as Necropolitans do not benefit from natural healing and can not receive long term care.


Necropolitan is a +0 CR Template that can be applied to any (no longer) living creature.

Template Traits

  • Size and Type: A Necropolitan’s Type changes to Undead. They experience no change in size.
  • Hit Dice: A Necropolitan’s Hit Dice remain unchanged from their base creature. Any Hit Dice gained from Character Level also remain unchanged.
  • Unlike other Undead, a Necropolitan maintains its Constitution score: The Necromantic processes that sustain them perform in ways that mimic the life they once had.
  • Unlike other Undead, Necropolitans are not immune to mind-affecting effects, Ability Score drain or damage, or effects which require a fortitude save.
  • Darkvision 60 feet (if the creature already has darkvision, increase the range by 30.)
  • Immunity to death effects, disease, paralysis, poison, sleep effects, and stunning, exhaustion and fatigue, nonlethal damage, and energy drain.
  • Like undead, Necropolitans do not heal naturally. While Negative Energy effects will heal them as normal, they also become vulnerable to positive energy.
  • Not at risk of death from massive damage, and is not destroyed until -Con HP.
  • Not affected by raise dead and reincarnate spells or abilities. Resurrection and true resurrection can affect willing Deathless. These spells turn the undead creatures back into the living creatures they were before becoming undead.
  • Undead do not breathe, eat, sleep, or age. With significant taxidermy they may retain the ability to whistle and play wind instruments. Their voices are not universally heinously terrifying to listen to.

Becoming a Necropolitan

       The process for becoming a Necropolitan is freely available from any Union-licensed necromancer, provided that you can pay for it. The process is slow and expensive- it cannot be rushed under any circumstances. There are three ways to qualify for being a necropolitan- being living and willing, living and unwilling, or recently dead and willing. No Union member will willingly force someone to become a necropolitan, but magical anomalies (and non-members) in the past have produced necropolitans against the will of a living person. However, most necropolitans willingly undergo the process as part of a planned lifestyle change.

    The process for becoming a necropolitan costs 5,000 gp. The ritual itself takes one full month and cannot be interrupted. During that month, the soon-to-be necropolitan cannot take any action or leave the location at which the ritual is taking place. For a living person undergoing this process, that is pretty much the sum of it- once the ritual is finished, the person is a fully-functioning undead and can go about their unlife freely. The ritual itself, however, can be hazardous. A significant portion of the ritual is devoted to making sure that the necromantic energies being used do not corrupt the identity of the person being converted- that they retain their personality, motivations, and alignment. If the ritual is interrupted or performed incorrectly, the subject risks permanent destruction of his or her body, or worse being raised as a mindless or insane undead creation.

    The process can be performed on a deceased body. The individual in question cannot have died from old age. The body must be well-preserved- use the rules for Raise Dead for the state of the body. The process of being "raised" in this manner is extremely traumatic. The ritual has a %5 chance of failure even if everything is performed perfectly. Even if it succeeds, the necropolitan gains a permanent negative level that persists even though they would be otherwise immune. Unlike a normal negative level, Restoration has a %50 chance of removing this level, and Greater Restoration removes it, if performed by a necromancer (Though it is quite uncomfortable.) Further, the physical trauma that caused the initial bodily death persists. With skilled taxidermy, the damage can be minimized but it will still be present, even if necromantic energy is used to heal other damage to the necropolitan.

Note: A character that is a Necropolitan at character creation does not have to pay the 5,000gp price.
