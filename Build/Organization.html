Organization

    Blackfeather's central office is located in Greygorge. Greygorge is an ancient canyon in the massive Bonepeak mountain range. Greygorge is a notorious quagmire. No one nation, even in time of war, has been able to hold onto it for more than a few years. As such, no nation officially owns Greygorge. Blackfeather sought to change this. A group of their best adventurers cleared the monsters out of an ancient abandoned Lycan fortress at the head of the canyon. They found that  the Fortress was constructed atop an aquifer, was several times larger than expected, and was even warmed by a deep geothermal system. Blackfeather immediately moved their base of operations to the fort, renaming it Blackfeather Fortress.


    The exact contents of Fort Blackfeather are a secret at the highest levels. The vast majority of the structure is not used in day-to-day operation. Massive sections of the complex are restricted, even to longstanding Blackfeather members. No official maps of the interior of the Fort exist. As such, there are endless rumors about what, exactly, Blackfeather hides in one of the most tightly secured locations in the entire world.


    Fort Blackfeather has been the company's central base of operations for decades. Given its extralegal status, it gives them a huge degree of flexibility in hiring and housing adventurers, as well as acquiring the materials and contracts they need. A small city has sprawled out of the fort, and now Greygorge is a fairly bustling community. Much of the economy in Greygorge is highly illicit, but Blackfeather uses its adventurers to police it heavily. They do not want a reputation for harboring international criminals. Still, Greygorge is a community where nearly anything can be found... for a price. As a note of trivia: Greygorge has more taverns-per-capita than any other city in the world.


    The rest of the Company is organized into offices- Each major city holds at least one small Blackfeather office. Larger offices serve as barracks and training grounds, where a number of adventurers can be kept on hand for rapid response to local problems. Company stores and wizarding services are also frequently available at these offices.


     If a contract needs to be made, a potential client files its paperwork at the local office. That office then sends it off to central, where central contacts appropriate adventurers. If transport to a location is needed, Blackfeather provides it. The Adventurers frequently use local offices as staging grounds, and return there once a contract is complete.


    The entire process is highly organized and efficient, so much so that some sarcastic individuals have started calling the offices "Embassies." This does not assuage the concerns of those who are suspicious of Blackfeather.


    Blackfeather also reportedly maintains groups of elite problem-solvers referred to as "Special Investigations" teams. These groups of high-level adventurers supposedly serve as investigators and troubleshooters for the Company as a whole. Whenever something threatens the Company itself, or a group of adventurers is in serious danger, or on jobs that are just too dangerous or important to give to anyone else, these elite teams are deployed. Blackfeather, of course, officially denies their existence.


    Blackfeather insists on treating its members as family. They encourage referring to fellow adventurers as if they are family. They believe that this creates the proper environment of loyalty and group membership that Blackfeather requires.
