Forged

Racial Traits

  • +2 to one Ability Score: Forged, being built rather than born, are always constructed with a purpose in mind.
  • Medium Size: Forged have no bonuses or penalties due to size.
  • Normal Speed: Forged move at base speed of 30 feet.
  • Living Construct: Unlike other constructs, a Forged has a Constitution score, makes Fortitude saves, and is not immune to mind-affecting spells or abilities. Forged are also at risk from death from massive damage,nonlethal damage, stunning, ability damage, ability drain, death effects, and necromancy effects.


  • Forged do have immunity to poison, sleep effects, paralysis, petrification, disease, drowning, fatigue, exhaustion, nausea, effects that cause the sickened condition, and energy drain. A Forged does not need to sleep, though a Forged wizard must still rest for 8 hours before preparing spells.


  • A Forged cannot heal lethal damage naturally, and must be healed magically. They can be affected by spells that target living creatures as well as by those that target constructs. Spells from the healing subschool and supernatural abilities that cure hit point damage or ability damage provide only half their normal effects to a Forged. A Forged is made of wood and (usually)ferrous metal, and is subject to spells that affect creatures made of or wearing these substances.
  • Armored Body: At 1st level, a Forged receives a bonus feat which it must use to select one of the Forged Construction feats to represent what materials its body was crafted out of.  As a Forged body is constructed of wood and metal, it can be enchanted as though it were armor. However, if a Forged has enchanted its body in this fashion, the enchantments of any other armor or robe worn will not work unless the Forged suppresses the enchantments on its body.
  • Arcane Matrix: A Forged’s body is embedded with softly glowing arcanic lines, runes, circuitry and Magicapacitors that give life to its otherwise inert materials. As a fortunate side effect, a Forged can flush power to these lines, causing its body to cast light as if it were a torch.  This does not discomfort the Forged in any way.  They may switch the light off and on as a free action.
  • Languages: Forged begin play speaking Common and Prime. Forged with high Intelligence scores can choose from the following: Dwarven, Draconic, Hobgoblin, Orc, Ork, or Skaplyndi.

Forged Construction Feats

  • Composite Plating: The Forged was created using the most common materials used for making Forged: A wooden musculature, with vulnerable areas protected by steel plates. This provides the Forged with an innate +3 armor bonus.  Being thinly layered, normal armor or clothing may be worn over this: However, only the highest armor bonus will apply.  
  • Greenwood: The entirety of the major moving components of this Forged have been carved from greenwoodwood, providing a sturdy organic superstructure. Living Bronzewood is affected normally by healing magic of all types, but does not have the resistance to sharpened blades that armored composite provides. Greenwood superstructures do not provide an armor bonus, and do not provide the healing rate typical of greenwood items, but Forged with this feat receive the full 100% hp healed from any healing spells cast on them. Forged composed of Greenwood are not affected by spells targeting metal, such as Heat Metal
  • Mithral: The Forged’s exterior has been covered with high-quality mithral plating, increasing its durability at the cost of some of its maneuverability. A wooden superstructure supported by mithral struts and lamellar, strip, or solid mithral plating provides the Forged with a +5 bonus to AC, at the cost of a -2 armor check penalty and 15% spell resistance. The Forged’s maximum Dexterity bonus is capped at +5. The Forged now counts as wearing medium armor, and cannot wear additional armor of any type; however, his mithral plating can be enchanted as if it were medium armor, at normal enchanting costs.
  • Adamantium: Rather than using plated wood, the superstructure of this Forged is composed of solid Adamantium plate, providing solid, durable protection in exchange for significantly limited maneuverability and speed. A wooden core supported by thick, solid Adamantium exterior plate provides the Forged with a +8 bonus to AC and DR 2/Adamantium, at the cost of a -5 armor check penalty and 35% spell resistance. Its maximum Dexterity bonus is now capped at +1, and its base speed is reduced to 20’. The Forged now counts as wearing heavy armor, and cannot wear additional armor of any type; however, his adamantium plating can be enchanted as if it were heavy armor, at normal enchanting costs.



Additional Forged Feats

  • Advanced Materials: Magical and alchemical advancements in construct design and manufacturing have provided the Primarchy with numerous potential modifications for Forged and similar constructs. Some of these modifications take advantage of the new "replaceable parts" organizational design to enhance repair in and out of the field, while other modifications are designed to allow a Forged to upgrade his superstructure and external plates for a variety of changing conditions. By itself, this upgrade allows nonmagical repair attempts--the Forged and/or any assisting personnel may make a Craft (construct) check to restore hit points. This check has a DC of 15 to restore 1 hp, with an additional +1hp per 5 points that the result exceeds the DC. Due to instabilities in the Arcane Matrix that can potentially destabilize a living construct's animation, attempts to repair a Warforged in this manner more than once a day increase the check DC by 10 each time, and checks that are failed by 10 or more result in 1 point of temporary constitution damage. In addition, lost limbs can be replaced at any Primarchy Forge at the cost of 7500 gp (approximately the same cost as a Regeneration spell).
    • Requires: can only be taken at 1st level
  • Living Steel: This Forged has had his composite plating enhanced with Living Steel external plates. These plates provide a stronger epidermis for the construct, and have some capacity to repair themselves, providing the Forged with (very) limited healing. Forged with this feat have an innate armor bonus of +4 (replacing the +3 from composite plating). In addition, Forged equipped with this upgrade that are currently below (their level x 3) hp regain 2 hp per day until they have (their level x 3) or more hp. This will not restore damaged limbs.
    • Requires: Advanced Materials
  • Elemental Steel: The composite plate for this Forged has been enhanced with several layers of enhanced steel purchased from the local Dwarven nations. These plates are imbued with special elemental energies, and provide some resistance to certain types of energy exposure. Forged with this feat gain 6 points of Energy Resistance in an energy type chosen when this feat is taken. This feat cannot be taken multiple times.
    • Requires: Advanced Materials
  • Variable Wavelength Matrix: This Forged's Matrix has been modified to allow increased control and variable colors and lighting effects. The Forged can control the amount of light produced by his Matrix, switching it between off, torch-level, or daylight-levels as needed, and can change the color of the light to any part of the normal spectrum. In addition, the Forged can change the light produced so that it is only visible to creatures using Darkvision, or rotate between colors at high speed. As a result of these changes to his Matrix, the Forged gains Darkvision 60'.
    • Requires: Advanced Materials
