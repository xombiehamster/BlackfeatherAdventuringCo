Highbloods

     Serpente Highbloods have much more snakelike anatomy than their lowblood relatives, and gain much more physical power from their heritage. Though they have long, snake-like bodies, their build is still not heavier than a lowblood, leaving them deceptively agile and light on their scales. However, they cannot match a legged compatriot in pure speed. Highbloods were traditionally the rulers of society for recorded history in the Drakwyn empire. In Serpeggiare, they form a sort of noble class- The Duke must be a pureblood, and all serving clergy of the Cult of Drakaulos must be highbloods (Though there have been many reports of unsanctioned lowblood clerics and oracles.) Highbloods make up about ten percent of the population. They live about twenty or thirty percent longer than lowbloods.

    It is still uncommon to see Highbloods outside of Serpeggiare or its direct territories. As a social rule, they prefer to play politics and banking. It is seen as unfit for a highblood to do the "commoner's work" of mercantilism, or worse adventuring. Still, some highbloods ignore the social norms and do as they will, though they are few and far between (And some have been cast out of their families.) It is seen as very respectable to join a Mercenary House, though, especially if one become a Spada. Among the upper classes, bloodline is seen as very important. Many take up their bloodline and gain magical aptitude as Sorcerers, but serious practitioners are few. Similiarly, learning Spada d'Lacrimas is also encouraged, but most are dabblers at best.



Size: Medium - Though they have very long bodies, Highbloods are very lithe and must keep much of their body on the ground. As such, they are medium sized creatures


Abilities: Con +2, Dex +2, Cha +2 - Highbloods are physically tough, and also extremely agile- both mentally and physically.


Movement: Speeds Climb, swim, normal, all 20- Using their long bodies to their advantage, Highbloods are at home in many environments. However, due to their heavier weight, they must use strength for climb and swim checks. 


Senses: low light, darkvision 60ft, scent - Serpente Highbloods have excellent senses


Natural armor and weapons: +3 natural armor, 1d4 bite with a dc 13 save for 1d2 con damage

Skills:  +4 perception, +4 stealth, +8 acrobatics - Physically, Highbloods are closer to snakes than bipeds- this gives them some significant advantages.


Serpent Form: Because they do not have legs and a large amount of their body is always on the ground, Highbloods cannot be tripped and gain a +2 to CMD and CMB for the pin maneuver while grappling as long as they are on the ground. Due to sheer overall mass, they count as large sized for several effects- including mounts and any ability that treats a large sized creature as two medium (such as teleport spells). They count as medium sized creatures for all equipment and carrying capacity purposes. They cannot wear boots or shoes. Their Boots magic item slot is taken up by tail rings.


Level Adjustment: +1
