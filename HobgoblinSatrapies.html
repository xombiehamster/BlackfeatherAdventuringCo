Hobgoblin Satrapies

Located predominantly in the lands South of the Southern Mountains, the Satrapies are the traditional lands of the Goblinoid Races.

    Within the Hobgoblin Satrapies, Hobgoblins are the dominant culture, being the first of the Goblinoids to successfully conquer and suborn their fellows. Over the long years, the Goblinoids have settled into a comfortable cultural contract with eachother. Hobgoblins are the primary laborers, handling industry, agriculture, the military, and of course the all important Trade of rare spices, minerals, and magical reagents native to their homeland that gave the Satrapies their wealth.

    Goblins fill the role of house servants, handling the menial tasks of the home, such as tidying, cooking, and so on, and some will assist their homeowners with tasks related to their profession. Although initially this practice came from the Hobgoblin conquest and enslavement of their smaller, weaker neighbors, in modern times it is recognized more as a form of caste structure, where a given Goblin family will typically form an unwritten contract of service for a given Hobgoblin family. While Goblins do have fewer rights and privileges, they are provided food, shelter, and frequently regular stipends.

    Bugbears are an odd case, as they are rarely seen even in the Satrapies. This is intentional, as Bugbears prefer not to be seen, shrouding themselves in secrecy and mystery. What is known is that Bugbears are spies and assassins without peer, and have served the Hobgoblin Satrapies in this capacity since the rise of the Great Sultans. Word has it, however, that in modern times the Bugbears no longer serve a single master, as rumors abound of feats of incredible spycraft in foreign lands.

    In modern times, the Satrapies, and Hobgoblins in general, have entrenched themselves as a primary conductor of international trade for many nations that would either be unable or, occasionally, unwilling to trade between each other, as they can act as a neutral and (mostly) fair third party. The Satrapies themselves play host to a wide variety of rare and useful reagents, alchemical ingredients, and exotic goods that serve as the backbone of the Hobgoblin trade empire.

    However, it's important to keep in mind that "trade empire" may be a bit of a misnomer: The traders and convoys that conduct trade across the mainland are often self-employed individuals, or occasionally employees of a more wealthy Satrap or Sultan. There is no centralized trade organization within the Satrapies, and many of them are more than willing to undercut another trader if it means more coins end up in their pocket. In addition, they are by no means the only traders around... but wherever traders may be found, one is likely to find a hobgoblin there.
