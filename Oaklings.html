Oaklings

For Humans, Elves, and many other species, forests and jungles will always hold a special place in their culture: being largely descended from such arboreal settings, they are recognized as places of succor, comfort, and beauty, as well as for their invaluable impact on the beginnings of industry and civilization. Artists, Poets, Lovers, and Druids the world over will attest to how serene, enlightening, and romantic a forest glen can be.


Many Oaklings, however, offer a different opinion.
    Oaklings are a species of humanoid plants that seem to possess many common features between plant and animal. Though similar in shape with other humanoids, they have a wooden complexion, a thick skin of bark, and roots, grasses, and leaves in the place of hair. Though they are called “Oaklings”, this is a misnomer, as many of them bear resemblance to Firs, Pines, Palms, and many other varieties of tree. Although having mostly human proportions, they have thicker trunks for legs, longer arms, and a neck so thick that many do not have a clearly defined jawline.

    The true origin of Oaklings, is somewhat mysterious, as not even the Oaklings themselves are aware of it. Many of them suspect that druidic fertility rituals (which many Druid sects are quick to deny), a number of “secluded glens”(which local townsfolk are quick to deny), and the mischievous intercession of Bronix (who is being typically coy on the situation) may be involved, but few of them are concerned enough to dig that deep into their own origins. 

    Perhaps one of the more curious elements of Oaklings is that they are “born” after growing for about 20 years: however, they become self aware before this, learning knowledge of local history and behaviour from the “forest memory” of the long-lived trees around them in a process that Oaklings call “boring”. The upshoot of this is that once they are mature enough to pull up roots, they will already have sufficient local knowledge to avoid all that awkward “fish out of water” behavior that gets so many other spontaneously aware species in trouble.

    Oaklings are not a very widespread species, but, oddly enough, the majority of them are typically found away from heavily forested areas: evidently, most of them feel that forests are “way too crowded” to be truly comfortable. Instead, they prefer cities or open farmlands, and can be found engaging in just about any occupation that takes their fancy. A fair number of them even turn to adventuring, as, after sitting motionless in a forest for about a decade, many of them are eager to stretch their limbs.

Racial Traits

Abilities: +2 to Strength, +2 to Wisdom, -2 to Dexterity: Strong and mentally resilient, Oaklings are nevertheless somewhat stiff and wooden, a pun they hate.
Medium Size: Oaklings have no bonuses or penalties due to size.
Normal Speed: Oaklings move at base speed of 30 feet.
Humanoid Plant: Oaklings, in spite of their appearance, are in many ways more animal than plant: As such, their type is considered to be Humanoid, although they also count as plants for the purposes of spells and effects.
Thick Bark: Oaklings receive +1 Natural Armor.
Plant Resistances: Although not immune, Oaklings receive some degree of resistance from their plant heritage against enchantment and poison. Oaklings have + 2 against Mind-Affecting effects and Poison.
Take Root: Oaklings have extensive experience in staying in one place. They receive + 4 on CMD vs bull Rush or Trip while standing on ground.
Phototrophic: Oaklings can heal themselves when exposed to sunlight. In sunlit conditions (magical or true), once per day as a standard action, the oakling can heal 2 points of damage per character level. Using this ability also provides the oakling with one meal’s worth of nourishment.
Treespeech: Much to their chagrin, Oaklings have a natural ability to commune with plantlife, although for the most part such conversations are really, really dull. They are under a continual Speak with Plants spell.
Languages: Oaklings speak Plant and Common. Oaklings with high Intelligence scores can choose from the following: Dwarven, Elven, Gnome, Halfling, Skaplyndi, Sylvan, Terran.
Oaklings eat, breathe, and sleep.
