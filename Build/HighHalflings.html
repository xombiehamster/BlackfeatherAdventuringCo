High Halflings

Found predominantly among the most populated areas of The Mainland, and in particular within the realms of the Patchquilt Kingdoms, High Halflings may be the best known subvariant of the Halfling peoples.  Taller than their cousins and of noble bearing, they hold themselves to be the "True" Halfling race, with other derivative Halflings being unfortunate offshoots.  Their argument for such largely has its foundation in the long and storied history of the High Halfling people, from their many independent High Halfling nations within the realms of the Patchquilt Kingdoms, to the many Halfling heroes that are historically considered to have been High Halflings (at least, as far as Arcane University confirmed records are concerned).  In addition, their greater stature, while not granting them any significant physical advantage over their kin, does point to them being closer to the proposed original Human/Dwarf Hybrids that are typically considered to be the progenitors of the Halfling race (although many High Halfling scholars currently argue that it is more likely that Humans and Dwarves are merely highly speciated Halflings, but have yet to present any compelling evidence in favor of this).  However, far more notable than the physical differences from their fellows, High Halflings are most easily differentiated by a stern culture of dignity, fealty, and a staunch military mindset with which they view the world.  Although they would not describe it themselves in such terms, the Culture of the High Halflings is one of a "Siege Mentality", beset on all sides by rivals or enemies who are larger, stronger, and more physically capable than they, and that it is only the unity, dedication, and righteous honor of their kind which can see them hold fast against any foe.  Because of this outlook, High Halflings are innately distrustful of the "Tallfellow" races, and in turn are considered to be more haughty and unapproachable than many of their more integrated kin.  This is not to say that they cannot cooperate with other races, for they are more than capable of engaging in trade, diplomacy, and joint warfare with other races, and once an alliance is brokered can be trusted to uphold it with staunch honor.


Individually, High Halflings tend to be fierce enemies or staunch allies, with very little spectrum in-between.  Once one has gained their trust (which is no easy matter for any who is not another High Halfling, let alone a "Tallfellow"), only a serious infraction tends to change such a determination, as High Halfling culture holds the defense of ones friends and family in high esteem.  If angered, however, High Halflings are slow to forgive, and while only the most severe infractions demand blood in return, such an offender will find themselves on a difficult road to rebuild relations.

  • Fleet of Foot
  • Underfoot
