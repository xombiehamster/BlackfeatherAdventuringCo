Welcome

Welcome, one and all, to the front page of the Manifest Destiny setting's infodump site! This setting is a collaborative effort between many individuals for nearly a decade, and as such it's understandably a massive quagmire for anyone new to navigate; fortunately, the setting tends to stick to most of the classic Fantasy Cliches, so if you've spent any amount of time in this hobby, you probably won't find many exceptions to how you expect things to work.  Furthermore, it's also pretty much a kitchen sink of all things fantasy, so there's room for just about anyone and anything and anyplace somewhere in the setting.  Probably the biggest distinctive differences in the setting can be summed up as such:

The world takes itself seriously.

I sure didn't.

As such, the exceptions to the classic fantasy cliches you'll run into usually are focused around parodies, satire, and occasionally just straight up goofy shenanigans that nonetheless the inhabitants must endure with stoic pride.  Whether it's Bureaucratic Wizards, Majordomo Goblins, Elven Mafias,  Socialist Dwarves etc etc etc, these poor blighters deal with it in stride.  

There's also one other distinctive difference worth mentioning; even after all this time, there are more than plenty blank holes in the setting, waiting for something clever to be inserted.  The setting is designed to be Kitchen Sink and fanciful, so feel free to introduce your ideas on what belongs in this crazy place!  Otherwise, read at your own pace, take everything with a grain of salt, and if you find yourself laughing at something, then I really hope I meant it to be funny.


If you're landing here for the first time, be warned that this site is currently a hybridization of old, 3.pf era stats that are being modified into 5e. In general, any fluff  you run across is correct(well, as far as anything is, we fully utilize the unreliable narrator framing here), but mechanics may not be.  In general, when you see a reference to something, check for that something in 5e to find what is most likely the correct rules.

Also, there's gonna be a bunch of holes, just saying.  Constantly under construction.  If you got an idea for what goes in that whole, float it! This is a patchwork setting on purpose, after all.

For anyone who wants a more ritzy introduction, I present this purple prose recovered from an old document;


The Year is... irrelevant.  A thousand different nations measure the passage of time a hundred different ways.  In some places, it is the year 5329 of their Kingdom’s long reign. To others, it is year 31 of their Emperor’s rule.  It is roughly a century since the Tortugatan Invasion, a thousand years since the Wizard War, two thousand since the fall of the Lycan Empire, countless millions since the gods first cast their gaze on The World.  To give a single year as though it were truth would be a grievous lie. Instead, better to recognize the truth: That, as in everything, time itself exists only in flux.

For, in truth, it is the whole of The World that is in flux.  Great kingdoms and powerful empires alike rise and fall, giving birth to a hundred smaller ones in their passing.  These in kind strive to consolidate their sibling societies, to reach once again the dizzying heights of their progenitor.  In the spaces between, new nations grow and die, their very existence an exercise in both Fate and Perseverance. Ancient societies and governments do everything to maintain the status quo, even as The World grows ever more complex, ever more detailed, ever more open.  New technologies bubble forth from Master and Maniac alike, and arcane lore grows ever more dense and dangerous. New lands, nations, species and spoils are found every decade, and each carries with it the anathema of inevitable change, every new discovery leading inexorably to an uncertain new future.  The self-appointed caretakers of The World scramble to keep it safe in the face of these new poisons even as the people they claim to protect embrace the change they bring.

And as The World changes, so too does it remain the same, as ancient enmities, grudges, insults and crimes percolate among the nations of the World.  Each King carries a dream of domination, each Emperor a vision of godhood, each Ruler a desire to better the lot of their people by any means possible. The World has simmered in a ceasefire enforced in secret by these aforementioned Protectors, but as their influence slips, so does their grasp on the floodgates which hold back the grisly tide of war.  Now, The World sits as a powderkeg, and for all their power, influence, and magic, No King, No Protector, nor even the gods themselves are able to dictate the ultimate Fate of The World. To their distress, that decision falls squarely on the shoulders of … everyone.

For no more is History dictated solely by the greatest of all nations, no longer is Truth determined by those who speak it the loudest.  The World is changing, and the actions by the meekest today may influence the greatest tomorrow, the Subjugated may come to Dominate, and all the nations of The World may be left broken, scarred, or altered in the wake of the History that is about to be made. But no single writer will pen that History, nor shall one single Author dictate it.  Instead, in this, the Time of Changes, the quill is placed in the hands of the most unlikely of people, each given free reign to change the face of history forever, and seize their own Manifest Destiny...
