Lamplighter

Lamplighter

Neutral (Chaotic?) Good

The Lamplighters have created an extremely odd system of belief that, were more people aware of it, might cause quite a number of metaphysical debates. The Lamplighter’s philosophy does not involve a deity of any fashion. Many Lamplighters worship other deities, mostly Drakaulos, but some reject the concept of worshiping a deity entirely. Instead, the Lamplighters are devoted to an ethos of reason, personal liberty, and what they consider enlightened philosophy. Somehow, they are able to draw divine power from these beliefs.

There are a few other religious movements such as the Walking Ancestors, the followers of Saint Errigal, or arguably the Primarchy that do not center on a specific deity, but both of these forms of belief involve some kind of mortal paragon or spiritual component. The Lamplighters base their philosophy on entirely mortal forces, yet somehow their devotion to these ideals has created a font of divine energy that they draw upon - even with the relatively short time that the organization has existed. Indeed, they may have found a way to “game” the cosmic mechanics of divine power and belief to grant themselves power.

As it is a philosophical ethos and does not specifically elevate any individual, the Lamplighters “deity” has a somewhat interesting influence on how their divine power works. Lamplighters who harness divine energy do so with all the trappings of a more religious institution, but they put their own odd twist to it. For example, their holy symbol is the same symbol used to mark their lodges on their maps of the underland. The symbol is frequently worked stealthily into more complicated engravings on weapons or tattooed on a Lamplighter somewhere discreet that can be revealed quickly - such as on the forearm under a sleeve. Their “deity’s” chosen weapons are firearms - a powerful weapon and example of the strengths of reason and progress.

Spells and effect that speak with the Lamplighter “deity” can be jarring to use. The Lamplighter voice is always a variant of the caster’s own voice, but frequently distorted. The user may hear their own voice as if they were much older, another species, gender, or even as if they were dead. It can be a disturbing experience, and the implications of this are unknown.

Regardless, outsiders know little of the details of how the “faith” of the Lamplighters works. They are extremely secretive about the nature of the divine power they wield, and indeed its very existence. They consider themselves on the side of good, but also consider the alignment to be somewhat constricted in its viewpoint. They almost always channel positive energy and usually assist other good aligned deities, but sometimes may be found in conflict with those who participate in governments that the Lamplighters see as “repressive.”

Holy Symbol: ⌆

Preferred Weapon: Firearms (Choose Musket, Pistol, or Coat Pistol)
Domains: Knowledge, Preservation, Liberation, Trickery
Subdomains: Deception, Espionage, Self-Realization, Freedom, Education, Thought
