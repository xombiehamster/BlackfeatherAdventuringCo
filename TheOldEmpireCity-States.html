The "Old Empire" City-States

    Approximately three hundred years ago, the Empire of Lock was one of the primary world powers, and a force to truly be reckoned with. Today, however, they are a pale shadow of their former selves. They have been locked in a generations-long war of succession that has waned from hot to cold, but left them long since a nation in name only. Today, they are merely referred to as the "Old Empire" in collective, and the "Human City-states" in passing. Despite the fact that all of the city-states are fiercely territorial and separatist, they remain inexorably economically linked.


    The Empire of Lock was formed from a human coalition that won many great victories during the Lycan wars. Humans, being far more physically and culturally diverse than most races, generally do not tend towards easy cooperation in large numbers, but the Empire was able to band them together under the banner of a stable government and strong leadership. During this era, Humans made themselves a world power. However, when the last king, King Jack the Bloodied, died without heirs, the nations finally collapsed.


    Today, each of the cities claim that their ruler is the one who deserves to sit on the throne at Colin's Bluff. In truth, however, few really believe that a unified human empire is possible again, or even honestly a good idea. Most believe that humans are simply too culturally diverse to manage such a wide-ranging government. Many of the city-states, such as New Highelf, have pretty much given up the Grand Game of succession entirely and decided to forge their own path.


    Between the city-states that compete for chunks of the Old Empire's power, most remain locked in a long and expensive power struggle. In reality, most of the actual military conflicts are fairly cut and dry. Large units of soldiers maneuver, there's maybe a clash or two, and terrain is given or lost by treaty. It is mostly for show and posturing, and mostly to have economic incentive for growth and keep their various populaces distracted.


    However, on a small scale, the conflicts can get pretty bloody. Most of the city-states vie for territory simply because of how economically important it is, not because they believe they are going to unify much of anything. Many of these territories, such as the human Islander nations, do not want to be part of an Empire and resent the conflict held over them. Occupying and fighting for some of these territories can be fierce. Small-scale conflicts and shadow wars break out from time to time among the nations, and these can get very bloody quickly, however they tend to stay on the small scale.


    The "Old Empire" was geographically one of the largest, and easily the most culturally diverse. It spanned from the northern mountain ranges, including New Highelf and bordering Greygorge, right up to the coastline of the inner sea. Human city-states are scattered around this entire region, even out into the coastal islands of the sea itself, and together constitute a very large population. It is because of this that most of the other nations tend to leave the potentially vulnerable city-states alone. Fractured as they are, the humans are not much of a threat. If something caused them to be unified again, such as an outside threat, they could once again be powerful enough to shake the world.
