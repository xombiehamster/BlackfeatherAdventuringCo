Alignment

  When referring to detection spells or abilities, Alignment refers to one's affinity to the various alignment planes. When a paladin detects evil, she is specifically detecting a person's affinity to the negative energy plane- characters whose minds stray towards thoughts of evil have a stronger affinity for this plane. The same goes for Good, Law, and Chaos. According to magical theorists, when a person has thoughts or conviction relating to concepts, it forms a minute connection to the plane with which that thought is affiliated. Magic, whether innate or learned, can be used to pick  up these connections- this is what detection spells find. It is also the reason why "false positives" can happen. Negative energy based undead, for example, always detect as evil due to their connections to the negative energy plane, regardless of their personal beliefs. This is also why good and evil clerics and paladins have auras and can channel energy from those planes- their convictions are strong, and they have great affinity for the planes. This only goes for law, chaos, good, and evil. Other planes do not seem to form a connection to mortal minds- a pyromaniac cannot be detected with "fire" alignment, for example. Whether sentient minds empower the planes or the planes influence mortals is the subject of fierce debate in academic circles.


    The ability to detect alignment has existed for all of recorded history. An alignment detection does not give you any true evidence of wrongdoing or honesty, it merely gives evidence as to the values that a person holds. Plenty of regular working folks detect as evil, for example. It doesn't mean that they are villains or criminals- though they may be unpleasant or pettily cruel. In the broad scope history, though trends exist, misguided "Good" forces have occasionally been responsible for several atrocities and "Evil" forces have actually bettered the world from time to time. As such, most people do not put a major emphasis on alignment detection to decipher another person's intentions. More specifically, most view it as one more factor in the complex tapestry of motivations that most people posses.


    Alignment does not dictate every action a person takes. They maintain multitudes of other motivations, including (but not limited to) love, loyalty, debt, friendship, profit, nationalism, survival, and fear. While alignment may color how an individual views each of these concepts, these motivations may cause a person to act in a way that might not appear in step with their alignment. This does not necessarily mean they are changing alignment, it is just part of the many minor compromises that go into life. Of course, alignments can change- numerous decisions add up, some slopes are slippery, and major life-altering events or decisions can cause someone to re-evaluate themselves. Aside from the average person, many individuals are legitimately devoted to good, evil, law, or chaos as concepts- perhaps as part of religious conviction, perhaps as part of their world view. Sometimes this can be taken to dangerous extremes.


    In general, when deciding what an individual's alignment should be, one should view everything that the character believes and holds important. Then, once who that individual is as a person has been decided, find the alignment that best fits the person. Alignment is only one factor in a person's decision making abilities- keep in mind the many other things a person holds valuable before assuming what they will decide. Alignment does not dictate actions, or indicate the morality of every action a person takes. Not everyone sees good as always positive and evil as always negative- different cultures have different values, and will see these alignments differently (If they care at all.)


Blackfeather Adventuring Company does not discriminate based on alignment. Any alignment is acceptable, as long as the individual demonstrates professionalism and loyalty to the company.

Traditionally, though, Chaotic Evil characters are started on a probationary period, and Chaotic characters aren't trusted with paperwork and valuables until they have proven their loyalty.
