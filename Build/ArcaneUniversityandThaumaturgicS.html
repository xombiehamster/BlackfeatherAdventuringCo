Arcane University and Thaumaturgic Society

The symbol of the Thaumaturgic Society.  

It is laced with mystery and menaces with spikes of hidden lore.


    Although many consider the two to be synonymous, technically they are two distinct entities, although in practice they may as well be one and the same.

    The Arcane University is the catchall organization that handles the teaching, registration, licensing, and research of the Arcane Arts. As large, monolithic organizations go, they are one of the most well known, omnipresent, and powerful ones in all the World. If a character has any kind of Arcane magic, it is extremely likely that they learned their craft at a University College, school, or by a University-ordained tutor. Anyone who did not is by and large considered to be dangerous, even by those who otherwise bristle at the University’s meddling: although they may not like their heavy handed nature, history has shown all too well the dangers of unregulated Magic. However, there is always an opportunity for someone who was not originally trained by the University to become a licensed practitioner, provided, of course, they can pass their rigorous examinations and contribute a not-inconsiderable amount to the University’s coffers. 


    The power-behind-the-throne is the Thaumaturgic Society. This organization, comprised of numerous powerful Wizards, Clerics, and other shades of Magic User, and composed of positively no one who cannot cast at least a 5th level spell (with exceptions made for those who run out before that), is dedicated to one, all important task: They are pretty much the lone decider of what is ‘Orthodox” in the realm of Magic, and are willing to put their considerable resources into maintaining this monopoly. In truth, the origins of the Society are actually quite noble: High level casters are capable of literally world shattering magics, and history has shown time and time again that when one individual has High or, even worse, Epic level magic available to them, its power is more often than not ultimately corrupting. The Society was founded to finally regulate and organize magic in order to put a lid on not only these world-threatening Aberrants, but in general to protect the ‘mundane’ people of the world from the fallout of magical misuse. It may be true that the current Society, however, has somewhat lost sight of their roots: In addition to using the University (whose High Magus is always a member of the Society) as a heavy-handed club to control and clamp down on Magic Users and applications that they deem “unorthodox”, the Society has grown rather Pro-Arcane in recent times. Although there are still many Divine(and otherwise) practitioners within the Society, they find themselves somewhat outnumbered: Unfortunately, the nature of their magic means that it is much more difficult for them to create such a unified organization as the University. The subsequent in-fighting and politicking has resulted in an organization that is now less concerned with protecting the non-magic users of the world, and more interested in making sure that their particular brand of Magic-Use is safe, not only from Aberrants, but also from other Practitioners. 


    In spite of these differences, the Society is quick to respond when they recognize something that could be a legitimate threat to all Magic Users, such as what, exactly, all those misfits are doing on Nova Island...
